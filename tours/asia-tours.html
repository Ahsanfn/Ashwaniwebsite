Travel tips for Asia trips

When to visit Asia on a tour?

Dreaming of an Asia vacation? There are many incredible spots to travel to on the world's biggest continent. Our guided tours of Asia can take you to many different countries including China, India, Japan, Thailand, and Vietnam. Whether a particular time of year or a destination is driving your travel plans, we'll help you find the tour that's right for you.

The climate varies greatly from region to region in Asia. Japan is temperate throughout the year and experiences four seasons. The monsoon season lasts from July to September in India, and it also tends to be rainy during the summer in China. Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam are warm and humid throughout the year. The best time to visit Southeast Asia is from November through February, when the temperatures are slightly cooler.

Where to go on a trip to Asia?

On each of our Asia tours, you'll have an expert Tour Director with you every step of the way. Local guides will also join your group at different points throughout your journey to help you make the most of your trip.

Looking for the best places to visit in Asia? Here are some of the incredible spots you can explore on tour:

  • The Great Wall of China
  • Mount Fuji in Japan
  • Sukhothai Historical Park in Thailand
  • Angkor Wat complex in Cambodia
  • Ha Long Bay in Vietnam
  • The Taj Mahal in India

We offer Asia tour packages for every type of traveler. If you'd like to visit China, for example, you can take our 15-day China: Beijing, the Yangtze & Shanghai tour or opt for a shorter Highlights of China: Beijing to Shanghai trip. Vietnam & Angkor Wat is another popular option for adventurers drawn to Southeast Asia.

Dining tips for Asia

Experiencing a new destination's cuisine is one of our favorite parts about traveling. Asian culinary traditions are tied to local ingredients, cooking techniques, and customs. On all of our Asia tour packages, we include select mealsâcarefully planned by our team of travel expertsâto give you a taste of the culture.

When you visit Asia, you'll have many opportunities to sample flavors that may be brand-new to you, from bahn mi in Vietnam to ramen in Japan to potato curry in India. Despite the differences in cuisine across Asia, you'll find that certain staples are used in many countries. Rice, soy, noodles, and spices are often used to create a balance of sweet, spicy, and savory in meals.

Packing tips for Asia

Let's face itâpacking for any trip can be tricky! Our best advice is to plan ahead and try to lighten your load. As you're prepping to go on one of our Asia trips, keep these pointers in mind:

  • It's a good idea to stick to one carry-on bag and one suitcase per person.
  • Bring lightweight layers for varying temperatures.
  • When inside Buddhist temples, your shoulders and knees should be covered as a sign of respect.
  • A comfortable pair of walking shoes is essential for when you're out exploring.
  • Sun protection, such as sunscreen and a hat, is important to have on hand in warmer destinations.
  • Don't forget your camera, too!
